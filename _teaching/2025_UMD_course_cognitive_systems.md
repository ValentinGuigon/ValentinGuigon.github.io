Course material for **NACS 645: Introduction to Cognitive Science (Fall 2025)** at the University of Maryland.
Sessions covered: Cognitive systems 1 (reasoning with heuristics and biases), Cognitive systems 2 (memory).

Required readings:

- Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1974). Judgment under uncertainty: Heuristics and biases. Science, 185, 1124â€“1131.
- Gigerenzer, G., & Brighton, H. (2009). Homo heuristicus: Why biased minds make better inferences. Topics in cognitive science, 1(1), 107-143.
- Roediger, H.L. III (1990). Implicit memory: retention without remembering. American Psychologist, 45, 1043-1056.
- Rugg, M.D. & Yonelinas, A.P. (2003). Human recognition memory: A cognitive neuroscience perspective. Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 7, 313-319.
